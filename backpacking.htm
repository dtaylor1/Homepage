Jul-Aug Whitney Portal 5-6 Hike 44.5 miles on the John Muir trail from Onion Valley to Whitney Portal. It requires a shuttle. This segment features the JMT's finest scenery and sets you up for a Mt. Whitney summit. Permits from the Whitney Portal are almost impossible to get but starting at Onion Valley lets you avoid the bottleneck. Log a pre-hike overnight at either Grays Meadow, Onion Valley, or Lower Grays Meadow. En route, recommended overnights are at Charlotte Lake or Lower Vidette Meadow, then Tyndall Creek Frog Ponds, then camp off-trail near Hitchcock Lakes and rise early for the 13-mile final day, which includes a 4-mile pack-free side trip to Whitney's summit before ending at Whitney Portal. Go mid-July to September for snow-free access to the alpine zone. Required permits begin March 1 for the following year.
